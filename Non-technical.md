# ROS 1 for Non-Technical Managers

Welcome to this simple guide to ROS 1 (Robot Operating System). This document is written for non-technical team members—like HR managers or business stakeholders—who want to understand what ROS is and why it matters.

## Overview
### What is ROS 1?
ROS 1 is a software framework that helps engineers build and control robots. It isn’t a physical robot or an operating system like Windows—it’s more like a toolkit that helps different parts of a robot (like its sensors, motors, and brain) work together smoothly.

You can think of ROS as the “glue” that holds a robot’s parts together.

## Why ROS 1 is Important?

- It helps teams build robots faster and more efficiently.
- It’s widely used in universities, research, and industry.
- It’s open-source (free to use), and supported by a large global community.

## Where Is It Used?
ROS 1 is used in many real-world areas, including:

- Warehouse robots
- Healthcare assistants
- Delivery robots and drones
- Educational robots in schools and universities
- Early-stage product development

## Key Takeaways
- ROS 1 connects robot components so they can work together.
- It’s mostly used for learning, research, and testing.
- ROS 2 is the newer version for industry use, but ROS 1 is still popular and useful.
